Nambi Narayanan

Nambi Narayanan (born 12 December 1941)is an Indian aerospace scientist who worked for the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). As a senior official at the ISRO, he was briefly in charge of the cryogenics division.

About the legand



    Birth: Born on December 12, 1941 in Nagercoil, Travancore


    Education: Earned a NASA fellowship and a master's degree in chemical rocket propulsion from Princeton University.


    Career: Worked as a payload integrator at the Thumba Equatorial Rocket Launching Station.


    Arrest: In 1994, Narayanan was arrested and charged with espionage and corruption.


    Investigation: The Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) found the charges to be baseless and exonerated Narayanan in 1996.


    Compensation: In 2018, the Supreme Court awarded Narayanan ₹50 lakh in compensation.
    In 2019, the Kerala government awarded him an additional ₹1.3 crore.


    Padma Bhushan: In 2019, Narayanan was awarded the Padma Bhushan, India's third-highest civilian award.


    Autobiography: Narayanan's autobiography is titled Ormakalude Bhramanapadham


    Film: The 2022 film Rocketry: The Nambi Effect, starring and directed by R. Madhavan, is based on Narayanan's life.
